MY PATH TO CODE

Hello, friend. I took a winding road in order to become a developer.
I have a unique perspective and a diverse set of experiences that make me an effective candidate for this profesion.

A Love for Creation

From the time I was 8 years old I knew what I wanted to be when I grew up. "Artist" is what I would tell people. The joy of making. It's a passion I hold dearly to this day.

Art isn't just a thing you do. It's a whole process. After years of projects, I can say with confidence that I'm well equiped to bring my imaginatinon to life with intent.

Restaurant Rockstar

Alright, 'rockstar' is a bit of an exageration. But for nearly a decade I thrived in the industry and worked every position you can imagine short of managing the entire establishment.

As a natural introvert, working directly with customers forced me to break out of my shy shell. I wouldn't trade the experience for anything because it taught me the personal growth that comes with getting outside of my comfort zone.

Technical Awakening

My search for better-paying, reliable work incedentaly led me to automotive. I was bit by the fix-it bug. Solving tricky problems and fixing vehicles felt like having magic powers.

What drove my success as someone who had no clue what they were doing was my ability to understand cars as a compilation of systems. Once I learned the 'why', I was able to develop a knack for diagnostics. There's a science to finding problems within increasing complex systems.

Love at First Type

After months of exploring career options I decided to try my hand at coding. From the first time I saw my name on the tab of a browser window my heart skipped a beat. I couldn't believe I had so much creative power at my finger tips. "Where has this been all my life," I wondered to myself.

I quickly discovered that development was a home where I could practice all my passions. With code, my artistic leanings are allowed to create beautiful and useful programs. There is never a shortage of new and interesting challenges to tackle, which also means there's no shortage of lessons in humility. I can't think of anything else I would rather do than work to build technologies that connect people and help make the world just that much better for the people that use that technology.
